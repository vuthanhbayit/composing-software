As far as I’m aware, the first book to popularize the term “transducer” in the context of general
purpose software-based stream processing was the 1985 MIT text book for a computer science course
called
“Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs”⁶²
(
SICP
) by Harold Abelson and Gerald
Jay Sussman, with Julie Sussman. However, the use of the term “transducer” in the context of digital
signal processing predates SICP.
Note:
SICP is still an excellent introduction to computer science coming from a functional
programming perspective. It remains my favorite book on the topic.
More recently, transducers have been independently rediscovered and a
different protocol
developed
for Clojure by Rich Hickey (circa 2014), who is famous for carefully selecting words for concepts
