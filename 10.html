You Compose Software Every Day
If you’re a software developer, you compose functions and data structures every day, whether you
know it or not. You can do it consciously (and better), or you can do it accidentally, with duct-tape
and crazy glue.
The process of software development is breaking down large problems into smaller problems,
building components that solve those smaller problems, then composing those components together
to form a complete application.
