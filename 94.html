Category Theory
A lot of functional programming terms come from category theory, and the essence of category
theory is composition. Category theory is scary at first, but easy. Like jumping off a diving board or
riding a roller coaster. Here’s the foundation of category theory in a few bullet points:
•A
category
is a collection of objects and arrows between objects (where “object” can mean just
about anything). Objects are like types in programming, meaning that they usually represent
sets of things with one or more elements.
